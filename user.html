What is an E-Challan?

Electronic Challan (E-Challan) is a digital traffic ticket issued for violations of traffic rules. Our automated system captures violations and generates digital tickets linked to your vehicle registration.

Important Notice

Pay your challan within 15 days to avoid additional penalties and legal consequences.

General Traffic Rules

  • Always carry valid driving license and vehicle documents
  • Use indicators when changing lanes or turning
  • Give way to emergency vehicles like ambulances and fire engines
  • Do not use mobile phones while driving
  • Always wear seatbelts in four-wheelers
  • Do not drive under influence of alcohol or drugs

Traffic Signal Rules

Red Light: Stop completely behind the stop line

Yellow Light: Prepare to stop or clear intersection if too close

Green Light: Proceed if intersection is clear

Helmet & Safety Regulations

All two-wheeler riders must wear helmets that meet safety standards:

  • Both driver and pillion rider must wear helmets
  • Helmet must have ISI certification mark
  • Strap must be properly fastened
  • Visor should be clean and unscratched

Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common traffic-related queries

How can I check my challan status?

Enter your vehicle registration number in the E-Challan section above. You can also check via SMS by sending VEH [space] your vehicle number to 5667788.

What happens if I don't pay my challan on time?

Unpaid challans can result in:

  • Additional late fees
  • Vehicle registration being blocked
  • Difficulties during license renewal
  • Legal proceedings in severe cases
How can I contest a challan if I believe it was issued incorrectly?

You can contest a challan by filing an appeal at your local traffic police station within 15 days of issuance. Bring all relevant documentation including your driving license, vehicle registration, and evidence supporting your claim.

Where can I pay my traffic challan?

You can pay traffic challans through multiple channels:

  • Online through our portal
  • Mobile payment apps
  • Designated banks
  • Traffic police stations
How long does it take to get a license renewed?

License renewal typically takes 7-14 working days. To expedite the process, ensure all your documents are in order and apply at least 30 days before expiration.
